Carrot

Carrots (Daucus carota) are a root vegetable, typically orange,
though they come in other colors like purple, yellow, red, and white.
Known for their crunchy texture and sweet, earthy flavor, carrots are not only
a versatile cooking ingredient but also packed with nutrients.

Nutritional Benefit 1

Rich in Vitamin A: Carrots are one of the best sources of beta-carotene,
which the body converts into vitamin A.
This is essential for eye health and helps prevent conditions like night blindness.

Nutritional Benefit 2

High in Antioxidants: Besides beta-carotene, carrots contain other antioxidants,
such as lutein and zeaxanthin, which help combat oxidative stress and reduce the
risk of chronic diseases.

Nutritional Benefit 3

Fiber: Carrots provide dietary fiber, which supports digestion and aids in
maintaining healthy blood sugar levels.

Nutritional Benefit 4

Vitamins and Minerals: They are also a good source of vitamins C,
K, B6, potassium, and biotin.
